Perfecting my Brioche buns: A journey. I am no great baker, and I tend to hyper-focus on certain recipes. It's been the art of sushi rice for a good two years, and now I want to make the perfect brioche burger buns.

## Ingredients

**Serves:** 18 buns

| Ingredient          | Amount  | Notes                |
| ------------------- | ------- | -------------------- |
| Whole milk          | 600 ml  | warm to 30C deg      |
| Yeast               | 40 g    | fresh                |
| Sugar               | 3 tbsp  |                      |
| Eggs                | 4       | Room temp            |
| Wheat flour         | 1200 g  |                      |
| Salt                | 2.5 tsp |                      |
| Salted butter       | 200 g   | **almost** room temp |
| Eggs (for brushing) | 2       |                      |
| Sesame seeds        |         | for topping          |

## Method

Warm the milk until it is lukewarm - testing 30C degrees at the moment. Dissolve the yeast in the warm milk, then add the sugar and eggs, and mix well.

Add 500 g of the flour and the salt, and knead the dough on a mixer at low speed until combined.

Cut the butter into small cubes. Add it to the dough gradually along with the remaining flour, a little at a time. Knead at medium speed for 8-9 minutes, until all the butter is fully incorporated and the dough is smooth, elastic, and pulls away from the sides of the bowl.

Cover and leave the dough to rise in a warm place for 1½ hours.

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and divide it into 18 equal pieces. Shape each piece into a round bun and flatten slightly. "Wrap" dough underneath itself for coherent top and avoid breakage when baking.

Place them on a baking tray lined with baking paper, and leave to rise for 1 hour.

Preheat the oven to 200°C.

Brush the buns with beaten egg and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake for 15 minutes - until golden and cooked through. Leave to cool on a wire rack.

## Obvervations

If using salted butter, which I prefer, room temp is a bit much. Take it out and cut into pieces, leave in fridge, until 30 minutes before use.
